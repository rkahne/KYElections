Kentucky Election 2015: Matt Bevin and the Medicaid Expansion

The graphic above shows the relationship between the vote for Matt Bevin and Jenean Hampton in 2015 and the Per Capita Medicaid Expansion. Those with a high proportion of votes for Matt Bevin and a large increase in Medicaid population are the counties in the upper right corner. These individuals could be seen to be voting against their own interests.

Update 11/7/2015

Somebody mentioned that there should be maps! So, I made some maps with choroplethr

Update 11/18/2015

The most requested map out of this project was something that ranked the correlation between Bevin Vote and Medicaid Expansion. I've done this using Z-Scores, which can be used to measure the correlation between variables. I had been wracking my brain to think of a way to do this, and was finally inspired by this post on fivethirtyeight.com. The map below measures the difference in z-score between Medicaid Expansion and Non-Bevin vote. The higher the difference between those two, the more a county can be said to be "voting against their own interest", based on what I said above.

Data for this project was retreived from the Kentucky Secretary of State's website and the Kentucky Report on 2014 Medicaid Expansion, prepared by Deloitte.

Produced with Plotly and R-Studio.
